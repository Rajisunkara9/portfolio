COMPETITIVE-ANALYSIS-OF-LEADING-TRAVEL-AGGREGATORS

This project aims to conduct a comprehensive competitive analysis of top travel aggregator platforms to evaluate their market positioning, strengths, weaknesses, and strategies. The analysis focuses on understanding how these platforms operate, attract users, and maintain their competitive edge in a highly dynamic industry. The study uses data-driven insights, customer sentiment analysis, and feature comparisons to highlight critical differentiators among key players like Booking.com, Expedia, Kayak, MakeMyTrip, and others. By leveraging tools like IBM Cognos Analytics, the project creates actionable insights that stakeholders can use to enhance decision-making, improve services, and increase customer engagement.
Market Positioning: Understand the relative positioning of leading travel aggregators in terms of market share and customer preferences. Feature Comparison: Analyze the services and features offered by competitors, including booking systems, mobile apps, and user interfaces. Performance Metrics: Evaluate website traffic, conversion rates, and customer satisfaction levels. SWOT Analysis: Identify the Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats for each competitor. Recommendations: Provide actionable insights to improve market positioning and customer experience for travel aggregators.
The project covers:

Generating a detailed report with charts, dashboards, and visualizations. Highlighting areas where each platform excels or needs improvement. By completing this analysis, stakeholders will gain: A clear understanding of how top travel aggregators compete and innovate. Insights into customer preferences and emerging market trends. Actionable recommendations to improve service offerings, attract more users, and drive growth.

MULTI PERSPECTIVE FRAUD DETECTION METHOD FOR MULTI-PARTICIPANT E-COMMERCE TRANSACTIONS

The primary objective of this project is to develop an advanced fraud detection framework specifically tailored for multiparticipant e-commerce transactions, with a focus on integrating. user behaviour analysis, anomaly detection techniques, and ensemble classification to enhance the accuracy and efficiency of fraud detection, ultimately fostering a secure and trustworthy online transaction environment.

By understanding typical user interactions within the e-commerce ecosystem, we establish a baseline against which abnormal behaviours can be identified. Subsequently, we delve into the analysis of abnormalities for feature extraction. Utilizing sophisticated anomaly detection algorithms, we scrutinize transaction data to uncover irregular patterns indicative of potentially fraudulent activities. This process allows us to extract important features that serve as key indicators for fraud detection.
Finally, we employ an ensemble classification model to implement our fraud detection mechanism, avoiding reliance on a specific algorithm. Instead, we leverage the strengths of ensemble algorithms, such as Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, or AdaBoost. By feeding the extracted features into the ensemble model, we train it to discern between legitimate and fraudulent behaviours in multiparticipant e-commerce transactions. Ensemble methods are particularly well-suited for this task due to their ability to handle high dimensional data and capture complex decision boundaries through the combination of diverse base models.
